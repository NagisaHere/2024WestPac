The Board and the Pieces
The first step to learning chess is understanding how the board is set up and what the pieces do.
Chess is played on an 8x8 board with 2 players. Each player is given 8 Pawns, 2 Rooks, 2 Knights, 2 Bishops, a King, and a Queen. The board here shows the starting setup of the pieces. Click on any of the pieces to see what it is and how it moves!
This is the rook. It moves straight, any number of squares vertically or horizontally.

You can try moving it yourself by clicking on the piece and then clicking on the board where you want to move it to.
This is the pawn. It moves 1 square up, but on its first move, it can move 2 squares up.

You can try moving it yourself by clicking on the piece and then clicking on the board where you want to move it to.
This is the knight. It moves in an L shape, and can jump over pieces.

You can try moving it yourself by clicking on the piece and then clicking on the board where you want to move it to.
This is the bishop. It moves any number of squares diagonally.

You can try moving it yourself by clicking on the piece and then clicking on the board where you want to move it to.
This is the queen. It can move straight and diagonally, like a rook and bishop combined.

You can try moving it yourself by clicking on the piece and then clicking on the board where you want to move it to.
This is the king. It can move 1 square in any direction.

You can try moving it yourself by clicking on the piece and then clicking on the board where you want to move it to.
